### Ingredients

**Aromatics & Oil:**
- 1 tbsp coconut oil or olive oil
- 1 cup yellow onion, diced
- 1½ tsp garlic (about 3 cloves), microplaned

**Spices:**
- 1¾ tsp ground cinnamon
- 1¼ tsp ground coriander
- ¾ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp red pepper flakes (or ¾ tsp for extra heat)
- 1½ tsp fine sea salt

**Liquids:**
- 2 cups vegetable broth (+ more for soupier consistency)
- 2 cups water
- 28 oz (1 large can) crushed tomatoes

**Main Ingredients:**
- 1 cup dry French lentils
- 2 cups carrots, sliced in thin coins
- 1½ cups cooked chickpeas (one 15 oz can, drained)

**For Serving:**
- 1 lemon
- Full-fat coconut milk or whole milk yogurt
### Instructions

1. Heat coconut oil in Dutch oven. Sauté onions 10 minutes on medium-low.
2. Add cinnamon, cumin, coriander, red pepper flakes, salt, and garlic. Cook 2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add broth, water, tomatoes, carrots, and lentils. Boil, then simmer 25 minutes. Add chickpeas and simmer 10 more minutes until tender. Thin with broth if needed.
4. Remove from heat, squeeze in half a lemon, and adjust salt.
5. Serve garnished with coconut cream or yogurt and cilantro. Pair with flatbread or couscous.
